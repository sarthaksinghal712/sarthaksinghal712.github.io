HKUST Canteen Experience

7th February 2023

Ever since the semester started, I've been super determined to work my best as having strong academic performance was my new year's resolution. I had my first class on Human Computer Interaction on 6th February 2023. It was a really interesting lecture as I always wondered about the heuristics that govern my choices when it comes to electronic devices. Well, this class was just what I was looking for. The course sparked in me the basic understanding of human perspective to computing. I'm super excited for this course, primarily because it's mostly project based, which makes it look so fun!

8th February 2023

Today's class gave us a brief introduction to what we have to do for our first group project in the team. The project is pretty exciting, we have to work on a the topic, "If ___ on campus were more user-friendly". Well, I wanted to stay on top of this, so while having lunch today at Passion, I spoke with a few friends of mine about what potential problems they have faced throughout their stay at UST. I got really interesting feedback from my friends. Quite a lot of problems at UST haha! I'm not much of a gymmer, but one of my friends is and he was so passionate about the need to reform the gym system. I felt so intrigued hearing that and was totally convinced that this is the topic I want to work on. But again, like I said, I'm not a gymmer, so I didn't relate strongly with the topic. Another friend of mine suggested that it's so annoying when they feel like studying and decide to sit in the library, travel all the way from their halls to the LC, only to find out that it's at capacity and there's no more seating available. Well, this problem could have been easily solved by counting the number of Student ID taps on their entry system, however, there's no tap done to count their exit so this won't be a possible project to work on. Another one of my friends suggested to improve the HKUST AirCon recharging system as it's a three step process which is pretty annoying, however, TKO Hall, in which I live in, has already simplified the system by making use of Octopus Cards instead.

15th February 2023

Today, I had an interesting interaction with one of my friends, he recently broke up with his girlfriend and were super sad as they also missed the deadline for USThing's Valentine's Match. He was very sad. While I consoled him, I also got the idea of reforming the USThing Valentine's Matchmaking by decentralizing the user profiles and making the match making process more transparent. I ended up pitching this idea to my groupmates which got all of us very excited as it clearly caters to the huge number of single people at HKUST. But since, a lot of my groupmates are exchange students and have no idea whatsoever about USThing's Valentine's Day Matchmaking, we had to drop the idea. We also discussed if we could work on UST's laundry system, as similar to the AirCon system, it's pretty outdated. But again, in my JCH, it's pretty convenient. So we decided to drop the idea.

24th February 2023

Based on the responses we collected, I was able to ideate another mindmap to fit this particular problem of non-user-friendly canteen experience. It looks like this: -

Based off the questionnaire, I was able to assess what kind of persona our users have, those who primarily belong to the student group. I came up with the following characteristics that define our user persona: -

  • Diet-conscious
  • Rushing
  • Allergic
  • Workaholics
  • In search for cheap and filling food

The questionnaire also allowed me to identify problems related to the poor dining experience. These included: -
  • Long waiting queues
  • Lack of seating space
  • No online ordering facility
  • Meals out of stock
  • No information about potential allergens

This also made me ponder what's the best way to implement this reform, should I create a new application, maybe create a virtual queuing system, what about work with just a single vendor, maybe just focus of availabiliy of seats system, etc. I realized that this all can be combined into a single page on HKUST Student's App which is already installed in most of our primary user's (students) devices. A simple task breakdown would invlove pitching a collaboration with HKUST Student's Application, collect statistics to validate our claims, collaborate with the canteen vendors, configure their POS systems to push information about stock to our API which we develop to standardize information transmission. And finally design a page in HKUST Student's App that can connect with this information. This clear approach of working systematically and getting to know your customers and their behaviour was pretty important to me as I was able to solve a lot of questions I was getting while I was simply thinking of the idea. Who better to ask for answers than the users themselves! We then started working on our storyboarding and presentation.
